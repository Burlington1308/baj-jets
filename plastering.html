Frequently Asked Questions

What is skimming?

Skimming means to apply a thin coat of finishing plaster to an existing wall or ceiling or to new plasterboards. Skimming creates a smooth finish to walls and ceilings before painting or applying wallpaper.

How soon can I paint my newly skimmed walls?

Please ensure your plaster is thoroughly dry before you consider decorating. The plaster will take a minimum of 3-5 days to dry thoroughly and in some cases longer. Once dry, the plaster will be pale pink and all brown patches will be gone.

How long does it take for plastered walls and ceilings to dry?

It typically takes about 2 to 3 days for plastered surfaces to dry, depending on several factors such as the thickness of the application, the type of plaster used, humidity levels, and temperature conditions. Thicker layers may take longer to dry completely. It's important to avoid drying it too quickly to avoid cracking as well as not rushing to paint or apply any finishes until the plaster is fully dry to ensure the best results.
